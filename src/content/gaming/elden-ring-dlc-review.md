The first hour of Shadow of the Erdtree tells you everything. A fog wall drops, a boss walks in, and it hits you twice while you're still figuring out where the camera went.

FromSoftware knows exactly what it's doing. The expansion doesn't waste time welcoming you back — it assumes competence and rewards it with some of the most intricate encounter design the studio has ever shipped.

## What Works

The new Scadutree system smartly gates power progression without locking content behind it. Explore, find fragments, get stronger. It's a loop that turns map traversal from busywork into active mitigation.

The weapon variety is the real headline. Backhand blades, throwing-star builds, a whole martial-arts moveset — this is a year of balancing distilled into a DLC drop.

## What Doesn't

Three of the legacy dungeons overstay their welcome. The castle in the northwest section recycles Elphael-style platforming without the visual payoff that made Elphael worth suffering through.

The final boss is good, not great. After what comes just before it, "good" feels like a small disappointment.

## Verdict

Essential. Even its flaws are the flaws of ambition.
