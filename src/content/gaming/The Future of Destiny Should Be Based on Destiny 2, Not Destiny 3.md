In the aftermath of Bungie's announcement (link announcement article) that Destiny 2 will no longer receive live service updates after the mammoth June 9th Monuments of Triumph update, the Destiny community has been clamoring for Destiny 3. With reports for Bloomberg and Forbes (hyperlinks) all but confirming that this is not currently greenlit or in development, a change.org petition sprouted up that at the time of writing has over 320,000 signatures, showing how resolved the community is in this goal to convince Sony to take a significant leap of faith in a franchise arguably on the downslope. Aside from the petition, the announcement started a wave of community discourse full of arguments, memories, art, clips, hope and pessimism. While not all positive, this amount of buzz serves as a reminder of how popular Destiny is in people hearts after a few years of decreasing player numbers.

Truthfully it's not just this announcement that has caused this wave of calls for Destiny 3, it's something that has been bubbling in the community for a few years now, and was especially amplified after the release of The Final Shape. While some were excited about the newly announced Fate Saga shepherding Destiny 2 into a new era, others felt The Final Shape was an appropriate time to leave Destiny 2 behind in favor of Destiny 3, and some felt that it was a satisfying conclusion to the franchise, and it was time to move on from Destiny as a whole. 

In this piece I want to tease out the arguments between a full fledged Destiny 3 with new systems, design philosophies, and story, and some form of continuation of Destiny 2's world, sandbox, and play structure. I am going to stretch the boundaries of what it means to be a continuation of Destiny 2 because clearly change is what the franchise needs. This is dicsusion is occuring because obviously the community believes Destiny needs more than just continued updates, and clearly Bungie and Sony do too. But the question is what should be the baseline for that change, because there are strong arguments for both.

The argument for Destiny 3 is simple, is rooted in the problems with Destiny 2, and is something many can just feel in their bones. Destiny 2 is old and full of baggage that holds back the development of the game and the acquisition of new players. Over the years some incredibly moments have taken place in Destiny 2, but alongside those triumps, tremendous mistakes were made. The removal of destinations and campaigns between 2020 and 2022, especially the introductory Red War campaign, made it practically impossible to onboard new players with a veteran to show them the ropes. In order to justify the continued grind for new gear a never ending power creep occured, resulting in a sandbox where our guardians can chain explosions to kill dozens of normal enemies in moments. I say normal enemies because in order to address our ever growing power, Bungie has had to stuff the game full of complex, tougher enemies to challenge us. Be it three types of champions, numerous enemies empowered with "Banes", or even simpler additions like tormentors and subjegators, the game is simply stuffed with proper nouns and rules that new players have to learn to be able to engage with the game. While these additions have helped address the growing malais casued by our strength, they have made the game cumbersome and confusing to all except the most commited players. I have tried to bring my more casual friends into the game and was met with confusion, leading to the first few gameplay sessions to be Destiny 101 lectures to be able to understand what the hell is going on. I know I'm rambling especially for those familiar with the current state of Destiny, but for lapsed or casual players, this should serve as some explanation of why the game is so impenetrable. 

Destiny 3 could solve all of this with a fresh start, limiting our power, resetting the grind, giving new life to the feeling of growth a new game can give you.

In addition to address structural issues within Destiny 2's gameplay and systems, Destiny 3 also can give a fresh start for Destiny's technical state. It has long been reporting that Destiny is a hard and costly game to develop, with legacy systems that are difficult to develop around. While Destiny's art style is second to none, aspects of the games engine are starting to look dated. I'm not a developer, so I won't pretend to know the how or why to what is occuring, but I know that developers want to build bigger things. More players, more enemies, bigger spaces, more ambitious encounters, interesting weapons, more vault storage solutions, a more alive world with regular changes to the play space.
