Summer is traditionally a graveyard. Publishers know it, players know it. But some years the calendar surprises you, and 2026 is shaping up to be one of those years.

Here are the five we're watching.

**Hollow Depths** — A co-op extraction shooter that actually cares about the extraction part. The demo footage showed verticality that most shooters in this space refuse to engage with.

**Meridian** — The third entry in a tactical RPG series that only gets better with each installment. If it ships in the state the previews suggest, it's GOTY-conversation territory.

**City of Ash** — Survival crafting, yes, but with a post-flood Pacific Northwest setting that no other game is touching right now.

**Project Icarus** — Still no release date confirmed. Still on this list. The concept is too interesting to ignore.

**The Pale Beyond 2** — The original was a quiet masterpiece of stress management and nautical dread. More of that is welcome any year.
