Every conference finals generates a set of narratives that calcify by tip-off and then get carried through the series regardless of what actually happens on the court. This year's matchups are no exception. Let's look at what the numbers say before those narratives fully harden.

## Eastern Conference

The team everyone expects to win this series should win this series. Their defense in the half-court ranks second in the league this postseason, and they're generating more corner-three attempts against switching coverage than any team in recent memory has managed.

The upset scenario requires their opponent's offensive rebounding rate to hold at what it did in rounds one and two, which is historically unlikely to sustain. These things regress.

Prediction: favored team in six, with real variance around game four.

## Western Conference

This one is legitimately open. Both teams defend at similar rates. The differentiator is transition — the team with the faster pace in the half-to-full-court transition creates significantly more points off turnovers, and this series will be decided in those fifteen-foot windows.

Watch the turnover rate for the slower-paced team's primary playmaker. If it stays under 3.5 per game, they can win. If it climbs above four, it's a sweep.

I don't have a strong lean. That's okay. Sometimes the right answer is "unclear."
